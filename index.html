My Approach To Productivity

The ideas on this page are from a variety of sources. The ones that I can remember are:

  • Atomic Habits by James Clear
  • Getting Things Done by David Allen
  • 100s of hours of YouTube/podcasts about productivity.

Main Ideas:

  • Systems are just as important than habits.
  • Anyone can and should change their habits
  • There are certain steps you can put into place today.

Systems

System: a set of things working together as parts of a mechanism or an interconnecting network (Oxford Languages). Habits do not live in isolation and you often need multiple habits working together to accomplish one goal. Let's take getting in healthy shape as a goal. What habits are required to accomplish that goal? You have to be adequate at time management, which is not a habit, but a system in itself, you have to eat the right foods, do the correct amount of exercising. But are there habits that will make those habits easier? Yes. What food you buy/ keep in the house is a habit that will effect this goal. If we are talking about buying something, it might require you to focus more on budgeting. Sleep is imperative for being in healthy shape, what habits factor into that? Not being on your phone to close to bed (keep it a decent distance from the bed?) Time management once again so you can get to bed on time. If you have any special requirements that you need in order to sleep well, those need to be in place; ear plugs? face mask? cold shower? hot shower? lighting? temperature? book?

Alright, I think you get the jist.

So how can we be more intentional about this? I would argue that an important step is self-awareness. What do you need in order to make the right decision? What cues will make you make the bad decision? What percentage of the time will seeing a donut on the table lead you to eating that donut? If it's a relatively high percentage, maybe you need to find a better location for the donut? Perhaps on the shelves at the store?

Long story short: If you just keep saying "I'm going to start eating right", but don't set up any additional helper habits that support that habit, you are significantly more likely to fail.

At this point you might be like "Jeez, this sounds like a lot of work, I'm just trying to live my life." In the last section, I will explain why this is actually not as bad as it sounds.

My Programming Journey

Stages so far:

  • Lack of Proper Structure in Learning
  • Some Structure, Lack Of Support
  • Perfect Structure,Plenty of Passionate Support

Lack of Proper Structure in Learning

For me, this stage consisted of two main approaches: attempting to use programming apps like SoloLearn, Mimo, Codecademy Go, and Grassopper AND/OR trying to following programming tutorials online. This stage lasted off and on for probably 3-5 years. Both approaches led me to quitting my latest attempt at learning to code. With the apps, I either got bored or wasn't putting the pieces together because instead of doing much coding, I was taking quizzes or doing fill in the blank coding. When it came to watching tutorials, I don't think I ever completed a single small project. I lacked the fundamental knowledge about programming languages and IDE's, so, with no one to help, I gave up

Some Structure, Lack Of Support

The next shift in my approach was when I committed to learning to code. I signed up for a university course. This experience was so much better because it spoon fed me the coding fundamentals, while also getting several small coding projects under my belt. But even this approach has its issues.
